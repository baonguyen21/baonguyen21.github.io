How to use this tool

Capacity rating of battery: Given on the listing or the outside of your battery. The unit for this is usually mAh for small battery
and Ah for a larger battery (like a car battery)

Current consumption of device during sleep: Measured between the battery terminal and the device at sleep to get the actual
consumption current. This current is more accurate than adding up all of the peripheral currents. Peripheral currents working
at different voltage and lost in DC-DC conversion can lead to the wrong current at the battery terminal.
How to measure current

Current consumption of device during wake: Similar to the current during sleep, between the battery terminal and the device at wake.
If the current varies during wake due to spike, one needs to calculate or estimate the average current draw when awake.

Number of wake up per hour: How many times does your device wake up and take in data. Twice every day = 2/24 wake per hour

Duration of wake time: How long does your device stay awake in 1 hour. Max value is 3,600,000 ms.
If your device wake up for 0.5 hours, twice everyday
Then wake time per hour = 0.5 * 2 * 3,600,000 / 24. This is in milliseconds so make sure your unit is right.

Discharge rate %/month: Battery will gradually lose its energy due to chemical reaction. Different type will have different
discharge rate base on their chemistry. See the table below for more information.

Battery System Estimated self-discharge
Primary lithium-metal 10% in 5 years
Alkaline 2-3% per year (7-10 years shelf life)
Lead-acid 5% per month
Nickel-based 10-15% in 24h, then 10-15% per month
Lithium-ion 5% in 24h, then 1-2% per month (plus 3% for safety circuit)

The Basic Formula:

C = Capacity rating of battery Milliamp hours (mAh)
Asleep = Current of the device when sleeping Milliamps
Awake = Current of the device when awake Milliamps
WakePerh = Number of wakeups per hour
WakeTime = Duration of a single wake Milliseconds
Adischarge = %/month * C / 100 / 730 Self-discharge current in Milliamps
3,600,000 How many milliseconds are in an hour
TimeWakePerh = WakePerh*WakeTime Wake time per hour
TimeSleepPerh = 3,600,000-WakeTimePerH Sleep time per hour
Aaverage = ((Awake+Adischarge)*TimeWakePerh+(Asleep+Adischarge)*TimeSleepPerh) / 3,600,000 Current on average (milliamps)
days = (Capacity/Aaverage)/24 life of battery, expressed in days
months = days/30.5 life of battery, expressed in months
years = days/365 life of battery, expressed in years
